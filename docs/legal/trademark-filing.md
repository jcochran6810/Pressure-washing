# Trademark search & filing — process guide

The name "Suds" is descriptive of the pressure washing industry, which makes it
**harder** to register as a federal trademark. You may want to consider a less
descriptive brand name OR file in a specific stylized form (logo + name) which
is easier to protect.

This guide walks you through (1) clearing the name, (2) filing in the right
class, and (3) protecting your domain and social accounts.

---

## Step 1: Free clearance search (do this BEFORE filing or printing materials)

Search the **USPTO TESS database** (https://www.uspto.gov/trademarks/search) for:

1. Exact phrase: `"SUDS"` in classes 35 (business services) and 42 (software).
2. Phonetic equivalents: `SUHDS`, `SUDZ`, `SUDSS`.
3. Same root: `SUD*`, `SUDS*`.

Also search:
- google.com — does anyone else use this brand for pressure washing software?
- Each major social network (X/Twitter, Instagram, LinkedIn, Facebook) — handle availability.
- Major business directories (Crunchbase, ProductHunt).

**If you find a registered mark in the same class with similar branding, stop and consult a TM attorney before going further.**

---

## Step 2: Decide the form of the mark

You can file:

| Form | Cost | Ease of registration | Protection scope |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Standard character (word) mark** | $250–$350/class | Hardest for descriptive marks | Broadest — protects the word in any font/color |
| **Stylized + design (logo) mark** | $250–$350/class | Easier | Narrower — protects only that specific visual |
| **Both** | 2× cost | — | Best protection |

For a name like "Suds" that's descriptive of cleaning, the **stylized + design**
filing is usually the more achievable starting point. You can add a word-mark
filing later if you can show "secondary meaning" (proof your customers
associate the word specifically with you).

---

## Step 3: Pick your classes

For a SaaS like this, the relevant Nice classifications are:

- **Class 9** — Downloadable software (less relevant; you're a SaaS not a download)
- **Class 35** — Business management services, advertising
- **Class 42** — Software as a Service (SaaS), software design and development
- **Class 38** — Telecommunications (if you're emphasizing SMS — usually not needed)

For Suds: **file in Classes 35 and 42**. Each class is a separate fee.

---

## Step 4: File

**Option A — TEAS Plus filing (recommended)**
- USPTO website → "Trademarks" → "Apply online (TEAS)" → TEAS Plus.
- Cost: $250/class as of 2024 (verify current fees).
- Must use pre-approved descriptions of goods/services — no custom wording.
- Lower fee, faster processing.

**Option B — TEAS Standard**
- $350/class.
- You can use custom descriptions but the examination is more rigorous.

**What you'll need:**
- Legal entity name and address (must match your registered LLC/Corp).
- A specimen of use — actual screenshots showing the mark in commerce. For SaaS:
  - Screenshot of your login page with the logo/name visible
  - A customer-facing invoice with the logo
- A signed declaration of use (TEAS handles this).
- Credit card.

**Timeline:**
- USPTO examines within **3–6 months**.
- If approved → published for opposition (30 days).
- If no opposition → registered (~12 months total).
- If office action (likely for descriptive marks) → respond within 3 months.

---

## Step 5: Maintain the registration

Once registered, you must file:

- **Section 8 declaration** between years 5 and 6 ($225/class).
- **Section 9 renewal** at year 10, then every 10 years ($425/class).

Set calendar reminders the day the registration issues.

---

## Step 6: Protect what you can without registration (do these immediately)

Trademark filing takes a year. Until then, protect the brand by:

1. **Buy the .com** — already done if you've followed earlier steps.
2. **Buy defensive domains** — `.app`, `.io`, `.net`, `.co` of your brand name. ~$15/yr each.
3. **Buy common misspellings and adjacent terms** — e.g. `getsuds.com`, `usesuds.com`, `sudsapp.com`. Even cheaper at registrars like Cloudflare or Porkbun.
4. **Register social handles** — even if you don't actively post, register the username on all major platforms to prevent squatters.
5. **Use ™ on the mark** — you can use ™ from day one even without registration. Use ® only after federal registration issues.
6. **Document first use date** — keep a screenshot or email showing when you first used the mark in commerce. This becomes important if you ever need to prove priority.

---

## Step 7: Consider a TM attorney (recommended for descriptive marks)

For ~$500–$1,500 flat fee, a TM attorney will:
- Do a more thorough clearance search (including state registrations).
- Advise on whether to file word, design, or both.
- Write the goods/services description for best chance of approval.
- Respond to office actions (which descriptive marks usually get).

Cost-benefit: if your brand becomes valuable, the $1,500 saves you a lot of pain
later. If the brand never takes off, you've over-spent. Decision rule: file
yourself if you're under 100 customers; hire an attorney by customer #500.

Find one: USPTO has a [registered attorney lookup](https://www.uspto.gov/trademarks/laws/find-registered-attorney).
Or use an online TM service: Cloudpeak, LegalZoom, Trademarkia (~$500–$700 + filing fees).

---

## Step 8: International filings (defer until $1M ARR)

If you ever serve customers outside the US:

- **Canada** — separate filing through CIPO. ~$330 CAD.
- **EU** — single application via EUIPO covers all 27 countries. ~€1,050.
- **Madrid Protocol** — file via USPTO, then designate countries. Cost-effective if you need 3+ countries.

Premature internationalization is a money sink. Wait until you have real
international revenue before filing abroad.

---

## A note about "Suds"

Strongly consider whether "Suds" is the brand you want to build a $20M+ business
under. Pros: catchy, memorable, on-theme. Cons:

1. It's a common English word for soap foam — descriptiveness makes registration harder.
2. Anyone can use the word "suds" in a generic sense without infringing.
3. There may be other small businesses or apps using "Suds" in adjacent industries (search before committing).

If you're early enough that rebranding costs are low, consider a coined or
arbitrary name (e.g., "Lumon", "Roam", "Float", "Pivot"). These register much
more easily and protect more broadly.

If you're committed to "Suds," compensate with strong visual branding (a
distinctive logo, color palette, typography) so the **design mark** has clear
visual identity.

- [ ] Decided word vs. design vs. both
