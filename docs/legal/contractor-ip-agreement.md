# Contractor IP Assignment & Confidentiality Agreement

**Template — for internal use only. Have an attorney review before signing with any contractor.**

This is the agreement you have any contract developer, designer, or freelancer sign **before** they start work. It assigns all IP they create to your company and protects your confidential information.

## 1. Engagement & services

Contractor will perform the services described in **Schedule A** attached
(&quot;Services&quot;). Contractor is an independent contractor, not an employee.
Contractor controls the manner and means of performing the Services.

## 2. Compensation

Company will pay Contractor as described in **Schedule A**. Payment is due
**[NET 15 / NET 30]** after invoice receipt. Late payments accrue interest at
1.5% per month.

## 3. Work product & IP assignment (the important one)

3.1 **&quot;Work Product&quot;** means all software, code, designs, documentation, data,
trademarks, artwork, content, inventions, and other materials Contractor creates,
develops, or contributes to during the Services, whether alone or with others.

3.2 **Assignment.** Contractor hereby irrevocably assigns to Company all
right, title, and interest in and to the Work Product, including all worldwide
intellectual property rights (copyright, patent, trademark, trade secret, moral
rights, and any other proprietary rights). Work Product is created as
&quot;work made for hire&quot; under U.S. copyright law to the maximum extent
permitted. Where the work-for-hire doctrine does not apply, the assignment in
this Section 3.2 effects the transfer.

3.3 **Pre-existing materials.** Contractor will identify any pre-existing
materials it incorporates into the Work Product in **Schedule B**. Contractor
grants Company a perpetual, irrevocable, worldwide, royalty-free, sublicensable
license to use, modify, and distribute those pre-existing materials as part of
the Work Product.

3.4 **Third-party materials.** Contractor will not include any third-party
materials in the Work Product without first informing Company and obtaining
written authorization. Contractor warrants that all Work Product is original
or properly licensed and that Company&apos;s use will not infringe any third party&apos;s
rights.

3.5 **Moral rights waiver.** To the extent moral rights cannot be assigned,
Contractor waives them with respect to the Work Product.

3.6 **Further assurances.** Contractor will execute any additional documents
Company reasonably requests to perfect the assignment in this Section.

3.7 **Open source.** Contractor will not use open-source components subject to
copyleft licenses (GPL, AGPL, LGPL) without Company&apos;s prior written approval,
which Company may withhold in its sole discretion.

## 4. Confidentiality

4.1 **Definition.** &quot;Confidential Information&quot; means any non-public
information disclosed by Company to Contractor, including source code,
customer lists, business plans, financial information, pricing, technical
specifications, and any information marked confidential or that a reasonable
person would understand to be confidential.

4.2 **Obligations.** Contractor will (a) not use Confidential Information
except as needed to perform the Services, (b) not disclose Confidential
Information to any third party, (c) protect Confidential Information with at
least the same degree of care it uses for its own confidential information
(no less than reasonable care), and (d) return or destroy all Confidential
Information upon Company&apos;s request or termination of this Agreement.

4.3 **Exceptions.** Confidentiality obligations do not apply to information
that (a) was already known to Contractor without confidentiality obligations,
(b) is or becomes public through no fault of Contractor, (c) is independently
developed without use of Confidential Information, or (d) is required to be
disclosed by law (with prior notice to Company where legally permitted).

4.4 **Duration.** Confidentiality obligations survive termination for **5 years**
(or perpetually for trade secrets).

## 5. Non-solicitation

For 12 months after termination of this Agreement, Contractor will not directly
solicit Company&apos;s employees, contractors, or customers for the purpose of
diverting them from Company. This does not restrict general advertising or
publicly posted job listings.

## 6. Representations & warranties

Contractor represents and warrants that:
- It has full authority to enter into this Agreement.
- The Services and Work Product will not infringe any third party&apos;s rights.
- It will perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner.
- It is not bound by any other agreement that conflicts with this Agreement.
- It will comply with all applicable laws, including export controls and tax laws.

## 7. Independent contractor status

Contractor is an independent contractor. Contractor is responsible for its own
taxes, insurance, and benefits. Nothing in this Agreement creates an employment,
partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship.

## 8. Term & termination

8.1 Either party may terminate this Agreement on **[30]** days&apos; written notice.

8.2 Either party may terminate immediately for material breach not cured within
**[10]** days of written notice.

8.3 Upon termination, Contractor will (a) cease all Services, (b) deliver all
Work Product (whether complete or in progress) to Company, and (c) return or
destroy all Confidential Information.

8.4 Sections 3, 4, 5, 9, 10, and 11 survive termination.

## 9. Indemnification

Contractor will defend, indemnify, and hold Company harmless from any third-party
claim arising out of (a) Contractor&apos;s breach of this Agreement, (b)
Contractor&apos;s negligence or willful misconduct, or (c) any claim that the
Work Product infringes a third party&apos;s rights.

## 10. Limitation of liability

Except for indemnification obligations and breaches of confidentiality or IP
provisions, neither party will be liable for indirect, incidental, special, or
consequential damages. Total liability is capped at fees paid under this
Agreement in the 12 months preceding the claim.

## 11. Miscellaneous

- **Governing law:** **[STATE]**, without regard to conflict of laws.
- **Disputes:** binding arbitration in **[CITY, STATE]** under AAA Commercial
  Rules, except injunctive relief for IP claims may be sought in court.
- **Entire agreement:** this Agreement and its Schedules are the complete
  agreement between the parties.
- **Amendments:** must be in writing and signed by both parties.
- **Assignment:** Contractor may not assign this Agreement; Company may assign
  to a successor in interest.
- **Severability:** if any provision is invalid, the rest remains in effect.
- **Counterparts &amp; e-signature:** this Agreement may be signed in counterparts
  and electronically.
